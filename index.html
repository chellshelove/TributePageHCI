Maya Angelou

Born Marguerite Annie Johnson

(April 4, 1928 – May 28, 2014)

Maya Angelou was an American author, actress, screenwriter, poet and highly influential civil rights activist who in literary history, wrote the first nonfiction bestseller by an African American woman. During her time she campaigned civil rights all across the United States

Top Works

  • ‘Still I Rise’ (1978)
  • ‘Phenomenal Woman’ (1995)
  • ‘I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings’ (1969)
  • ‘On the Pulse of Morning’ (1993)
  • ‘Life Doesn’t Frighten Me’ (1993)

    • Achievements

    Angelou received several honours throughout her career, including the National Medal of Arts in 2000, and the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 2011, Pulitzer Prize nomination for her book of poetry, and three Grammys for her spoken word albums

    Legacy

    When ‘Caged Bird’ was published, Angelou was hailed as a new kind of memoirist, and was placed in the tradition of African-American literature as a defense of Black culture. Her poem, encouraged Black feminist writings in the 1970s to "open themselves up without shame to the eyes of the world." Now, Angelou's poetry has influenced the modern hip-hop music community, including artists such as Kanye West, Tupac Shakur, and Nicki Minaj.
